FINANCIAL INVESTMENT PRINCIPLES AND STRATEGIES

Asset Allocation by Age
- Age 20-30: 70-80% Equity, 20-30% Debt
- Age 30-40: 60-70% Equity, 30-40% Debt
- Age 40-50: 50-60% Equity, 40-50% Debt
- Age 50-60: 40-50% Equity, 50-60% Debt
- Age 60+: 20-30% Equity, 70-80% Debt

Emergency Fund
- Should cover 6-12 months of expenses
- Keep in liquid instruments: Savings account, liquid mutual funds, FDs
- Example: If monthly expenses are ₹50,000, emergency fund should be ₹3-6 lakh

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) Benefits
- Rupee cost averaging: Buy more units when prices are low
- Disciplined investing: Automatic monthly investments
- Power of compounding: Long-term wealth creation
- Start early: ₹10,000/month SIP at 12% returns for 30 years = ₹3.5 crore

Expected Returns (Long-term)
- Equity (Large-cap): 12-15% CAGR
- Equity (Mid/Small-cap): 15-18% CAGR (higher risk)
- Debt funds: 6-8% CAGR
- Fixed Deposits: 6-7% CAGR
- Gold: 8-10% CAGR
- Real Estate: 8-12% CAGR (varies by location)

Risk Management
- Diversification: Don't put all eggs in one basket
- Risk tolerance: Conservative, Moderate, Aggressive
- Time horizon: Longer horizon allows higher equity allocation
- Rebalancing: Review and rebalance portfolio annually

Goal-Based Investing
- Short-term goals (1-3 years): Debt funds, FDs
- Medium-term goals (3-7 years): Balanced funds, hybrid funds
- Long-term goals (7+ years): Equity mutual funds, stocks

Retirement Planning
- Target corpus: 25-30x annual expenses
- Example: If annual expenses are ₹12 lakh, target corpus is ₹3-3.6 crore
- Start early: Starting at 25 vs 35 can make 2-3x difference in final corpus
- Use retirement calculators to determine monthly SIP needed

Tax-Efficient Investing
- ELSS for tax saving with equity exposure
- PPF for tax-free returns
- Health insurance for tax deduction and protection
- Home loan for tax benefits on interest and principal

Debt Management
- Debt-to-income ratio: Should be < 40%
- Priority: High-interest debt (credit cards) first
- Home loan: Good debt if within affordability
- Avoid: Taking loans for consumption
