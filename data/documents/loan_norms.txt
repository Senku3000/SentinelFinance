LOAN NORMS AND AFFORDABILITY GUIDELINES

Home Loan Eligibility
- Loan amount: Usually 80-90% of property value
- Down payment: 10-20% of property value required
- EMI affordability: Should not exceed 40-50% of monthly income
- Example: If monthly income is ₹1.5 lakh, maximum EMI should be ₹60,000-75,000

EMI Calculation Formula
- EMI = [P × R × (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N - 1]
- P = Principal loan amount
- R = Monthly interest rate (annual rate / 12 / 100)
- N = Loan tenure in months
- Example: ₹50 lakh loan at 8.5% for 20 years = EMI ~₹43,000

Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratio
- For property value up to ₹30 lakh: Up to 90% LTV
- For property value ₹30-75 lakh: Up to 80% LTV
- For property value above ₹75 lakh: Up to 75% LTV

Interest Rates (Approximate)
- Home loan: 8.5-9.5% (varies by bank and credit score)
- Personal loan: 10-24% (high interest, avoid if possible)
- Car loan: 8-12%
- Education loan: 7-12%

Loan Tenure
- Home loan: Up to 30 years (longer tenure = lower EMI but higher total interest)
- Personal loan: 1-5 years
- Car loan: 1-7 years
- Education loan: Up to 15 years (including moratorium period)

Prepayment and Foreclosure
- Most banks allow prepayment with minimal charges
- Prepaying reduces total interest burden
- Consider prepayment if you have surplus funds and no better investment options

Credit Score Impact
- Good credit score (750+): Better interest rates, higher loan approval
- Maintain: Timely EMI payments, low credit utilization, long credit history

Tax Benefits on Home Loan
- Interest deduction: Up to ₹2 lakh under Section 24(b)
- Principal repayment: Up to ₹1.5 lakh under Section 80C
- Total possible deduction: Up to ₹3.5 lakh per year

Affordability Check
- Total EMI (all loans): Should not exceed 50% of monthly income
- After EMI, should have enough for expenses and savings
- Factor in: Property maintenance, insurance, property tax

Loan Documentation
- Income proof: Salary slips, ITR, bank statements
- Property documents: Sale deed, title clearance
- Identity proof: Aadhaar, PAN
- Address proof: Utility bills, rental agreement
