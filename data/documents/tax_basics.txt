INDIAN TAX BASICS - KEY SECTIONS AND DEDUCTIONS

Section 80C - Tax Deductions (Up to ₹1.5 Lakh)
- ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Scheme): Invest in mutual funds, lock-in 3 years
- PPF (Public Provident Fund): Long-term savings, 15-year lock-in, tax-free returns
- Tax-saving Fixed Deposits: 5-year lock-in, interest taxable
- NSC (National Savings Certificate): 5-year tenure, interest taxable
- Life Insurance Premium: Premiums paid for life insurance policies
- Principal repayment of home loan: Principal component of EMI
- Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana: For girl child, 21-year lock-in

Section 80D - Health Insurance Premium
- Self, spouse, children: Up to ₹25,000
- Parents (below 60): Additional ₹25,000
- Parents (above 60): Additional ₹50,000
- Total deduction possible: Up to ₹1 lakh

Section 24(b) - Home Loan Interest Deduction
- Self-occupied property: Up to ₹2 lakh interest deduction
- Let-out property: No upper limit on interest deduction
- Principal repayment eligible under Section 80C

Section 80G - Donations
- Donations to specified funds: 50% or 100% deduction
- Examples: Prime Minister's Relief Fund, approved charities

HRA (House Rent Allowance) Exemption
- Minimum of: Actual HRA received, Rent paid minus 10% of salary, 50% of salary (metro) or 40% (non-metro)
- Requires rent receipts and PAN of landlord if rent > ₹1 lakh/year

Standard Deduction
- ₹50,000 for salaried individuals (from FY 2023-24)

Long Term Capital Gains (LTCG)
- Equity: 10% tax on gains above ₹1 lakh (holding > 1 year)
- Debt: 20% with indexation (holding > 3 years)
- Gold: 20% with indexation (holding > 3 years)

Tax Slabs (FY 2024-25)
- Up to ₹3 lakh: Nil
- ₹3-7 lakh: 5%
- ₹7-12 lakh: 10%
- ₹12-15 lakh: 15%
- Above ₹15 lakh: 30%
